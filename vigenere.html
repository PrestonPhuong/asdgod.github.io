How the Vigenère Cipher Works

  1. Write your message (plaintext) and choose a keyword.
  2. Repeat the keyword to match the length of the message.
  3. For each letter in the message:
    • Find the row starting with the plaintext letter (first column)
    • Find the column starting with the corresponding key letter (first row)
    • The intersection gives you the encrypted letter (ciphertext)
  4. To decrypt, find the key letter row and look for the ciphertext letter in that row. The column letter is the plaintext.
Example: Encrypting "HELLO" with key "KEY"

Message: HELLO

Key (repeated): KEYKE

Encryption:
H (7) + K (10) = R (17)
E (4) + E (4) = I (8)
L (11) + Y (24) = J (9)
L (11) + K (10) = V (21)
O (14) + E (4) = S (18)
Result: RIJVS

Note: The Vigenère cipher was invented in the 16th century and remained unbroken for centuries until Charles Babbage and Friedrich Kasiski independently developed methods to break it in the 19th century.
